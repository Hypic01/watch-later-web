How it works

Three steps, about five minutes

01

Add the Chrome extension

YouTube blocks apps from reading Watch Later, so the reading happens in your own browser. Install the extension, sign in, and your whole list syncs in one click. On Chrome, Edge, Brave, and Arc. On any other browser you can paste your list in by hand instead. Large lists can take several minutes.

02

The librarian sorts everything

Every video gets read and filed: worth learning from, worth watching, music, just for fun, or outdated, each with a one-line reason. Re-file anything and it learns your taste for next time.

03

Clean up, and stay clean

Mark videos done or dismiss the dead weight, and you get a checklist of everything that's safe to remove from the real playlist. From then on the extension files new saves on its own, so your library never rots back into a graveyard.

Privacy

Your account stays yours

We never see your YouTube password, cookies, or account. The extension runs in your browser, asks for access to youtube.com and nothing else, and only reads your Watch Later playlist. What we store: your email and the video titles you chose to import. What we never do: modify your playlists, read your watch history, or sell data. Delete everything with one button, any time.
